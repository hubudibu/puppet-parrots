ZuZu and ZaZa Puppet Parrot Ambassadors

ZuZu and ZaZa are puppets who teach with Children for Health staff on field trips in Africa. They help raise health awareness among children.

Donate

Why use the puppets?

Children can speak confidently through the puppets to tackle issues that may be too sensitive otherwise, like malnutrition and HIV.

The parrots also encourage young boys to help out at home by ZuZu being a responsible older brother to ZaZa, his younger sister.

The puppets can take on any role to help raise health awareness.

How the puppets are reaching children in Mozambique?

ZuZu and ZaZa were first introduced in January 2014. A dozen of puppet parrot pairs were distributed to 12 pilot schools in Tsangano, Tete Province. A camera was given to each school to document the programme.

By May 2015, the documented programme is a great success and keeps developing.

Guideline on how to teach with ZuZu and ZaZa

  1. Give the children a health topic to build a story around
  2. Ask children to role-play the story with the parrots.
  3. After the dialogue let children ask ZuZu and ZaZa questions.
  4. Let children answer the questions with the help of their teachers.

Read an example story

ZaZa is washing her beak in water. ZuZu comes along and see what she is doing.  ZuZu says dirt and germs are invisible and if you want to properly clean them you have to use soap. Soap is wonderful too it makes you smell fresh!

…the dialogue continues about how to persuade parents to buy soap. A further dialogue can be about using a tap and soap for hand washing that uses little water.
